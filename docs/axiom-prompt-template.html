English only - all sections and sub sections ie 1.1, 1.2, 2.1, 2.2 must be answered completely with max word count:

Objective: Provide a comprehensive, multi-disciplinary analysis for "[Project Name/Scenario Title Here]", addressing the core challenges and complexities outlined below.

Expected Standard: Your response should demonstrate graduate-level scientific/technical understanding, critical analysis, and in-depth reasoning for each section and sub-question.

Response Structure: Address each numbered section and sub-section clearly and sequentially, adhering to the provided headings.

Key Requirements for Each Answer Component:

  • 1. Justification: Support all claims, proposed methods, and conclusions with established principles, relevant theories, and (where applicable) supporting data or models.
  • 2. Depth: Elaborate on underlying mechanisms, complexities, nuances, and interdependencies. Avoid superficial or overly general statements.
  • 3. Critical Evaluation: Discuss trade-offs, limitations, assumptions, potential challenges, and alternative perspectives for all proposed solutions or analyses.
  • 4. Precision: Use accurate and specific terminology appropriate to each discipline. Define key parameters and metrics clearly.
  • METICULOUSLY FOLLOW THE PROMPT STRUCTURE: This is paramount. All sections must be answered, Do not skip sections or reorder them. Use the exact headings provided.
  • Address EVERY Specific Point: "Specific Points for In-Depth Discussion/Elaboration" or similar. Treat these as a checklist. Ensure each one is explicitly discussed in your response for that sub-section.
  • Focus on "Why" and "How": Don't just list techniques; explain why a particular technique is chosen, how it works in this context, and critically evaluate its trade-offs and limitations as requested.
  • Depth over Breadth (if forced to choose): If time/token limits are a concern, it's better to deeply answer fewer sub-sections fully than to superficially answer all of them while missing critical details or entire sections. (However, the goal is to answer all fully).
  • Self-Correction against Prompt: Before finalizing, re-read the prompt section by section and compare it against your drafted answer for that section. Have you met all explicit requirements?

How to Use This Master Template:

  1. Define Your Project: Clearly name your project/scenario (e.g., "Project Terra Firma," "Project Neptune Deep Dive").
  2. Select Disciplines: Choose 3-4 core disciplines that are central to your project.
  3. Craft Contexts: For each discipline, write a concise context statement linking it to your project's goals.
  4. Develop Challenges (1.1, 1.2, etc.): For each discipline, create two high-level challenges or problem areas.
  5. Flesh out "Specific Points": This is the most crucial part. For each challenge, list 3-4 specific, guiding bullet points that force the AI to delve into details, justify choices, compare alternatives, and discuss complexities. Tailor these points to the specific knowledge you want to test within that challenge.
  6. Review against AXIOM Principles: Ensure your questions demand justification, depth, critical evaluation, and precision.
This template provides a robust framework. The real art will be in crafting those "Specific Points for In-Depth Discussion" to perfectly target the nuances of the problem you want the AI to solve for each new AXIOM Inquiry.
